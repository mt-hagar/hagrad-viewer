ROI Tools

`ROI Circle` starts from the initial click as the center. Drag outward to size it, release to create it, then left drag inside to move it or drag the single outer handle to resize it.

Right-click an existing circular ROI to show its edit state.

`ROI Brush` paints a threshold-aware continuous ROI. Hold the mouse button down to paint, then use `Grow +` or `Shrink -` to refine it.

`Eraser` uses a round brush. It trims ROI contours and brush ROIs, and removes other annotations it touches.

`ROI Draw` traces freehand while you hold the left mouse button, then closes when you release. `ROI Multiple Click` places discrete points and finishes on double-click, then smooths the final contour so it does not keep polygon edges.

`Adjust ROI` refines the selected ROI contour. Start on or just beside an existing ROI, it will auto-select it, then redraw the part you want to replace and release to blend the correction into the ROI.

Selection
Draw a line or square profile to see the curve and edge metrics here.

In stent mode, you can drag the colored guide lines on the curve to fine-tune the cutoffs manually. The measurements update automatically.

`Stent Line` and `Stent Square` quantify the stent-lumen interface. `Plaque Calcified` and `Plaque Noncalcified` quantify plaque-lumen interfaces from contrast lumen into plaque.

Right-click any annotation to edit it directly.

The profile panel now reports stent-style metrics: strut peak HU, strut FWHM, lumen FWHM, and 10-90% edge sharpness.

Export

PNG, cine, and CSV output

`Export Cine Clip` saves a short movie of the whole stack. The file type depends on what your browser supports.

`Export Measurements` downloads one combined PNG plus a CSV that opens in Excel.

Before measurement export, HAGRad asks for a `Study ID`. That value is written into the PNG header and becomes the first column in the measurement CSV.

`Export Baseline Characteristics` creates a structured CSV from the loaded DICOM headers, reconstruction metadata, computed scan geometry, and any dose or contrast reports that were included in the loaded files.

Shortcuts & Help

Editable key map for daily use
Action Shortcut
Loading shortcuts...

Edit the `Shortcut` cells to fit your workflow. Single-key shortcuts are customizable here and saved locally on this Mac. Reserved keys such as `Space`, `Escape`, and `+/-` stay fixed for viewer navigation.

Fixed shortcuts stay the same: `Cmd/Ctrl+Z` undo, `Shift+Cmd/Ctrl+Z` redo, `Cmd/Ctrl+C` copy ROI, `Cmd/Ctrl+V` paste ROI, `Delete` removes the selected annotation.

Mouse wheel scrolls slices. `Cmd/Ctrl+wheel` zooms toward the cursor. Click a viewport to focus it. Arrow keys scroll the focused viewport. `Cmd/Ctrl+←` and `Cmd/Ctrl+→` switch loaded reconstructions. Space starts cine in the presentation stack.

In 4-up MPR, grab the crosshair center or colored plane lines directly.

Loaded reconstructions share one MPR coordinate system, so switching reconstructions keeps the same position, rotation, and zoom.

`Load DICOM Files` lets you select normal `.dcm` files. `Load Folder` is there if you want to pick one whole patient folder at once.

## Short overview section

`HAGRad was developed to support cardiovascular imaging research workflows that are often difficult to perform in standard presentation tools or generic viewers. The platform combines image review, annotation, export, and specialized quantitative modules in a flexible local environment that keeps data processing close to the user.`

## What HAGRad includes

### HAGRad Viewer
`A general research viewer for cardiac CT with image review, measurements, export, and multi-planar reformation tools.`

### EAT Workflow
`A dedicated epicardial adipose tissue workflow for contouring, thresholding, review, and structured export.`

### Coronary Workflow
`A coronary-focused research module for vessel-based review, curved reformation concepts, and quantitative annotation.`

### QCA Workflow
`A research-oriented workflow for invasive coronary angiography review and quantitative coronary assessment development.`

### Stent Viewer
`A stent-focused workflow for advanced reformation, lumen assessment, profile analysis, and per-stent export.`

### CalcScorer
`A coronary calcium scoring workflow designed for vessel-based Agatston-style research analysis and export.`

## Why HAGRad

- `Built for research`: supports exploratory workflows, iterative refinement, and structured export
- `Local by design`: image analysis runs locally instead of requiring cloud upload
- `Modular`: different cardiovascular workflows can evolve as separate tools under one umbrella
- `Presentation-aware`: designed not only for measurement but also for figure generation and scientific communication

## Research-use statement

`HAGRad is intended for research, education, and retrospective scientific analysis. It is not intended for routine clinical decision-making or regulated medical use.`
